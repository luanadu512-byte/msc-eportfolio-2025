ðŸ“Œ Project Summary

My MSc dissertation project focuses on the Application of Large Language Models (LLMs) for Electronic Health Records summarisation and patient search.
The objective was to build a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) clinical Q&A system for patient record summarisation and information retrieval.

  • Methods
    1. Used MiniLM embeddings to vectorise clinical notes.
    2. Applied Chroma/FAISS vector databases for similarity search.
    3. Built the full RAG pipeline with LangChain, including document chunking, embedding, retriever, reranker, and generation.
    4. Developed interactive interfaces with Streamlit and Gradio to simulate clinical information retrieval scenarios.
    5. Evaluated performance on the EHRNoteQA dataset using Accuracy, F1, and RAGAS metrics.
  • Outcomes
    • Achieved ~60% accuracy on the MIMIC-IV dataset (14,000+ patients).
    • Demonstrated the feasibility of RAG for EHR-based summarisation and clinical Q&A.
    • Established a baseline for future improvements (e.g., reranker integration, knowledge graph, multimodal data).
  • My Contribution
    I independently handled the entire pipeline: data preprocessing, system design, evaluation, and interface development.
    This process significantly enhanced my technical, analytical, and research skills.

âš¡ Challenges and Solutions

  2. Data: Long EHR texts contained redundancy and noise, lowering retrieval precision.

    Solution: Adopted a multi-dimensional evaluation framework with quantitative + qualitative analysis.

    Solution: Used weekly task lists and a Kanban tool to monitor milestones.
